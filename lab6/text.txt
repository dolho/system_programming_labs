<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 106px;">

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Manon_Melis" title="Manon Melis">Manon Melis</a></b> (born 1986) is a Dutch manager of <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_association_football" title="Women&#39;s association football">women's football</a> development at <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> and a former professional footballer who played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a>. She spent most of her professional career playing in the Swedish league <a href="/wiki/Damallsvenskan" title="Damallsvenskan">Damallsvenskan</a>, which she won three times with her club <a href="/wiki/FC_Roseng%C3%A5rd" title="FC Rosengård">LdB FC Malmö</a>, in 2010, 2011, and 2013. Melis also won the <a href="/wiki/Svenska_Supercupen_(women)" title="Svenska Supercupen (women)">Swedish Supercup</a> with Malmö in 2011, and was the Damallsvenskan top scorer three times, in 2008, 2010, and 2011. At age 17, Melis made her debut for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Netherlands women&#39;s national football team">Netherlands national team</a> in 2004. Her goalscoring helped the Dutch qualify for their first major tournament, the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Women%27s_Euro_2009" title="UEFA Women&#39;s Euro 2009">UEFA Women's Euro 2009</a>, where they reached the semi-finals. In the tournament, she scored a crucial goal in the final group game against <a href="/wiki/Denmark_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Denmark women&#39;s national football team">Denmark</a> to send her team into the knockout stage. When she retired from playing international football in 2016, she had earned 136 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> and scored 59 goals. She was the all-time top goal scorer for the Netherlands national team from 2010 to 2019. (<b><a href="/wiki/Manon_Melis" title="Manon Melis">Full&#160;article...</a></b>)
</p>

<p>This historical <b><a href="/wiki/Seal_of_Indiana" title="Seal of Indiana">coat of arms of Indiana</a></b> is an illustration from <i><a href="/wiki/Historical_armorial_of_U.S._states_from_1876" title="Historical armorial of U.S. states from 1876">State Arms of the Union</a></i> by Henry Mitchell, published by Louis Prang in 1876. The sun rising over the <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_Mountains" title="Allegheny Mountains">Allegheny Mountains</a> suggests that <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a> has a bright future. The woodman represents civilization subduing the wilderness, while the <a href="/wiki/American_bison" title="American bison">American bison</a> represents the wilderness fleeing westward away from the advance of civilization. This design is also used on <a href="/wiki/Seal_of_Indiana" title="Seal of Indiana">the state seal</a>, introduced in 1816, the year in which Indiana became a U.S. state.
